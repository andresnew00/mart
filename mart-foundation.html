Our Story

From the stages of
Brooklyn to Tel Aviv to London

Founded in 2018 by producer Sofia Kapkov, MART began by championing independent artists from Russia. When Russia invaded Ukraine in 2022, MART took an immediate stand, halting all Russia-based projects and redirecting its mission to support bold contemporary artists across the United States, Israel, and Europe. Today, MART produces work at venues like Brooklyn Academy of Music, Sadler's Wells, Festival d'Avignon, and The Public Theater, connecting the most daring living artists with audiences who are ready for them.

The Problem

Great Art Is Being Left on the Table

01

The Most Vital Artists Have No Pathway to American Stages

World-class choreographers and directors from Israel, Europe, and beyond are making work that is stunning, urgent, and politically resonant. Without a producing partner, most of that work never reaches New York or the global audiences it deserves.

02

Co-Productions Are the Only Way to Make It Happen

The gap between an idea and an opening night is enormous. It takes co-producers willing to share risk, source venues, and navigate international logistics. Without that infrastructure, brilliant projects stay in rehearsal rooms forever.

03

Cultural Exchange Gets Defunded First

Government arts funding has shrunk. Corporate sponsors follow big names. The emerging directors, the boundary-pushing dance companies, the cross-cultural theatre collaborations: these are always the first to lose funding, and the last to recover.

04

Art That Crosses Borders Makes Us All More Human

When Sharon Eyal's choreography premieres in New York, or an Israeli play about memory and history reaches London audiences, something shifts. People who see themselves reflected in art from another country stop being strangers. That work matters now more than ever.

Why MART

MART exists because the best art in the world should not stay local. We produce and support the most inventive voices in contemporary dance, theatre, and music, bringing them to audiences in New York, Europe, and Israel.

Choose Your Monthly Amount

Questions

What people ask
before they give

Where does my donation go?
Your gift supports MART's core work: co-producing and bringing contemporary dance, theatre, and music to audiences in New York, Europe, and Israel. This includes co-production costs, artist fees, venue partnerships, and the operational capacity that makes international projects possible.
Is my donation tax-deductible?
Yes. MART Foundation is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization based in the United States (EIN 61-1865805). All donations are tax-deductible to the fullest extent allowed by law. You will receive a confirmation email with the information you need for tax purposes.
How do I cancel or change my monthly gift?
You can cancel or adjust your monthly donation at any time with no penalty. Simply email info@martfdn.org and we will take care of it immediately. We want giving to MART to feel easy and right, not locked in.
Why does MART focus on international artists?
The most boundary-pushing work in contemporary performing arts often comes from outside the United States. Artists in Israel, Europe, and beyond are creating theatre and dance that is urgent, politically resonant, and rarely seen by American audiences. MART exists to close that gap by co-producing and supporting international tours and US premieres.
What is the Curtain Circle?
The MART Curtain Circle is our community of monthly donors. Members receive advance notice of new productions, impact updates connecting their giving to specific projects, and the personal satisfaction of knowing they are part of the producing infrastructure behind every MART show.
Can I give a one-time gift instead of monthly?
Absolutely. While monthly giving creates the most predictable support for our work, we warmly welcome one-time donations of any size. Every contribution is appreciated and used directly to support contemporary performing arts programs.
How does MART choose which artists and productions to support?
MART's founder and director Sofia Kapkov has spent her career building relationships with the leading artists and institutions in contemporary performing arts globally. Productions are selected based on artistic ambition, the strength of the artist's vision, and the opportunity for cross-cultural exchange that brings new audiences together.
Will I be able to see the shows MART produces?
Many of MART's productions are presented at major venues in New York such as Brooklyn Academy of Music, The Public Theater, and Classic Stage Company. Curtain Circle members receive advance notice of upcoming shows so they can plan to attend and experience the work their support makes possible.
How can I get involved beyond donating?
You can sign up for MART's newsletter, follow us on Instagram and Facebook, share our events with friends who love contemporary art, and spread the word about upcoming productions. Building an engaged audience community is as important as financial support, and we value every person who becomes part of our world.
Is MART's financial information publicly available?
Yes. As a US-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit, MART files annual Form 990 returns which are publicly available through ProPublica's Nonprofit Explorer and similar databases. We are committed to transparency and welcome any questions about our finances at info@martfdn.org.
